Welcome to Sizopi A04

Discover the wonders of wildlife in our natural conservation park

Experience the Wild

Sizopi offers an unforgettable adventure where visitors can observe animals in their natural habitat. Our park is committed to wildlife conservation, education, and providing an immersive experience for all visitors.

Our Facilities

Swimming with Dolphin

Experience the thrill of swimming alongside these intelligent marine mammals. Our trained dolphins offer an interactive adventure that creates memories to last a lifetime. Learn about marine conservation while enjoying this unique encounter.

Sea World

Dive into an underwater adventure at our Sea World attraction. Explore vibrant coral reefs, encounter exotic marine species, and watch spectacular aquatic shows. Our aquarium exhibits showcase the diversity of underwater ecosystems from around the globe.

Tiger Show

Witness the power and majesty of tigers in our educational tiger show. Our skilled trainers demonstrate natural tiger behaviors while educating visitors about conservation efforts to protect these magnificent endangered creatures in their natural habitats.

Baby Zoo

Delight in our Baby Zoo where children and adults can interact with adorable baby animals. This hands-on experience provides a gentle introduction to wildlife for young visitors, with opportunities to feed and pet friendly juvenile animals in a supervised environment.

Oriental Circus

Experience the magic of our Oriental Circus featuring talented performers from across Asia. This spectacular show combines acrobatics, animal demonstrations, and cultural performances in an entertaining and educational format for the whole family.

Taman Burung

Stroll through our Bird Park (Taman Burung) where hundreds of bird species from Indonesia and around the world create a symphony of colors and sounds. Walk-through aviaries let you get up close to exotic birds in lush natural settings.

Area Reptil

Explore our Reptile Area featuring various reptilian species from around the world. Get a close look at snakes, lizards, turtles, and the famous Komodo dragons. Our educational exhibits highlight the importance of these often misunderstood creatures in our ecosystem.

Kebun Kupu-Kupu

Enter our enchanting Butterfly Garden where hundreds of colorful butterflies flutter freely around you. Witness the complete lifecycle of these delicate creatures from caterpillar to chrysalis to beautiful butterfly in this magical tropical garden setting.

Safari Journey

Experience a thrilling drive-through safari where you can observe animals roaming freely in their natural habitats. Get up close with lions, tigers, elephants, and more from the safety of your vehicle or our safari bus.

Istana Panda

Visit our Panda Palace to meet these gentle giants from China. Our state-of-the-art facility replicates the pandas' natural habitat, allowing visitors to observe these rare and endangered animals as they play, eat bamboo, and relax in a carefully controlled environment.

Park Map

Navigate our expansive safari park with ease using our interactive map. Plan your visit to ensure you don't miss any attractions!

Animal Fun Facts

Sumatran Tiger

The Sumatran tiger is the smallest surviving tiger subspecies and is only found on the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

Did You Know?

Sumatran tigers have the most stripes among all tiger subspecies, helping them blend perfectly into their dense jungle habitat. With fewer than 400 left in the wild, they are critically endangered.

Komodo Dragon

The Komodo dragon is the largest living lizard species, native to the Indonesian islands of Komodo, Rinca, Flores, and Gili Motang.

Did You Know?

Komodo dragons can eat up to 80% of their body weight in a single feeding. Their saliva contains over 50 strains of bacteria, making their bite deadly even if prey escapes the initial attack.

Orangutan

Orangutans are great apes native to the rainforests of Indonesia and Malaysia, found only on the islands of Borneo and Sumatra.

Did You Know?

Orangutans share approximately 97% of their DNA with humans. They're the most arboreal of the great apes, spending nearly their entire lives in trees and building new nests each night for sleeping.

Javan Rhino

The Javan rhinoceros is one of the most endangered large mammals in the world, with only about 75 individuals surviving in the wild.

Did You Know?

Javan rhinos are so rare that many wildlife biologists have never seen one in person. They are extremely shy and can be distinguished from other rhino species by their single horn and skin folds that give them an armored appearance.

Bali Mynah

The Bali mynah, also known as the Bali starling, is a critically endangered bird endemic to Bali, Indonesia.

Did You Know?

With fewer than 100 birds remaining in the wild, the Bali mynah is one of the rarest birds in the world. Their striking white plumage and blue eye patches made them popular in the pet trade, contributing to their endangered status.

Sumatran Elephant

The Sumatran elephant is one of three recognized subspecies of the Asian elephant, and native to the island of Sumatra.

Did You Know?

Sumatran elephants are the smallest of the Asian elephants and have relatively brighter skin. They can consume up to 150kg of vegetation daily and drink as much as 200 liters of water. Their population has declined by 80% in less than 25 years.
